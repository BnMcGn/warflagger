
# WarFlagger Reference Guide

So you need to post a reference. Any URL can be used in a pinch, but some are much nicer. This guide is here to help you choose the better kind.

## Qualities

### Pre-existing and pre-vetted

Is your topic already on WarFlagger? Add to an existing conversation where possible. Save the trouble of merging two conversations later.

### Authoritative

Does your item of discussion have an authoritative source? Does your quote come from a longer interview or blog post? Where was that statement originally made?

Beware of MSM articles. They often clip statements, sometimes deceitfully. Links to the original are seldom provided.

We, the community, want to judge the context of a statement ourselves. Please do your best to facilitate this.

Try to find the whole, uncut interview. If you can't get the whole thing, post what you have. We can, in dire cases, reach out to the interviewee for amendments and confirmation.

For video evidence, try to locate the longest uncut version. Confirming alternate angles are also appreciated.

### Clean

On the other hand, it is good to face single issues cleanly.

You don't want a clear statement of truth to be muddied by downvotes because it is embedded in a longer article of mixed quality.

So maybe you have a brilliant statement that is embedded in a rambling two hour interview. Provide a link to that statement. Then, in a separate reference, tie the statement to the full interview for the sake of completeness.

With scientific papers, refer to the abstract. Make sure that a link to the full paper is available.

### Not Wikipedia

On which note: Wikipedia articles are not a great fit for WarFlagger, at least for the present.

First, they are in flux, making it hard to target excerpts within them. They are also a poor match for WarFlagger's discussion system. We prefer to break discussions out into a tree, in which each reference is weighed and rated.

This goes for encyclopaedic articles in general.

### Accessible, Public and Permanent

Please don't link to main pages of aggregators or other feeds. Find the permalink!

Non-paywalled articles are preferred, as are pages without excessive ads, popups and clutter.

Is your reference on a small, obscure site? We don't want our discussion to go away. Consider registering the site with an archiver such as archive.org.

### Transcript

We will want to discuss your reference. Does it have a clean text? In the case of video or audio, is a transcript provided? Locate one, if possible.

## Books

Conventions for book discussions have not yet been established. Here are some suggestions.

-   Textual versions that can be excerpted are preferred. Try [Gutenberg](https://gutenberg.org)
-   Publically available. See [Archive.org](https://archive.org/)
-   Standardized. We could use links to the Library of Congress or, perhaps, Amazon.
